Overview

Shade LA is a virtual reality experience designed to raise awareness about the importance of shade, hydration access, and urban design in downtown Los Angeles. The project simulates the physical and psychological challenges individuals face when navigating heat-exposed environments, particularly highlighting inequities between sheltered and unsheltered populations.

The experience combines first-person gameplay with a top-down city planning perspective, allowing users to both experience and influence urban conditions.

Core Concept

Players alternate between two modes:

First-Person Perspective (VR)
Experience daily survival and well-being challenges as different types of city inhabitants.
Top-Down Planning Mode
Modify the city environment to improve conditions such as shade availability, hydration access, and overall public well-being.

The goal is to understand how infrastructure decisions impact different populations over time.

Perspectives (Playable Roles)

Each round assigns the player a specific role with different constraints:

Sheltered Rider
Sheltered Pedestrian
Unsheltered Rider
Unsheltered Pedestrian
Key Differences
Sheltered individuals can enter buildings to reduce heat.
Unsheltered individuals have limited access to indoor spaces and must rely on environmental resources.
Each role experiences the city with different levels of difficulty and access.
Gameplay Loop

Each round consists of two phases:

1. First-Person Survival Phase

Players must complete three core tasks:

Sustain self (e.g., find water)
Enjoy something (e.g., activity or leisure)
Resolve a conflict

Players navigate the environment while managing physical and emotional conditions.

2. City Planning Phase

Players switch to a top-down view and implement improvements such as:

Adding shaded areas
Increasing hydration access
Adjusting urban layout

These changes affect future rounds and all player types.

Progression Structure
Round 1 – Sheltered Rider (introductory experience)
Round 2 – Unsheltered Pedestrian
Round 3 – Sheltered Rider
Round 4 – Sheltered Pedestrian

This sequence emphasizes contrast between privilege levels and lived experiences.

Player Interface
Wrist UI displays current objectives (task list)
City Overview allows zooming between macro (planning) and micro (character) levels
Core Systems
Stats (Visible & Hidden)

Visible Stats

Overheating (visualized as increasing intensity, e.g., fire effect)

Hidden/Derived Stats

Happiness
Dehydration
Energy

Happiness is influenced by multiple factors:

Heat exposure
Hydration level
Energy level

Environmental exposure directly impacts gameplay difficulty.

Environmental Mechanics
Overheating increases with time spent outside shade
Dehydration increases over time and is reduced by finding water
Energy is affected by both overheating and dehydration and influences movement speed

These systems simulate real-world stressors in urban heat conditions.

Design Goals
Highlight the importance of shade infrastructure in urban environments
Demonstrate inequities in access to cooling and shelter
Encourage empathy through embodied experience
Connect short-term events (e.g., LA28 Olympics) with long-term urban planning issues (e.g., tree aging, climate impact)
Social Context

The project is informed by real-world observations in Los Angeles, including:

The psychological impact of encountering unhoused individuals in public spaces
The lack of accessible shade and cooling infrastructure
The intersection of urban design and public health
